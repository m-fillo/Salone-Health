Salone Health Watch is a free, open digital tool that helps communities recognise common illness signs early, learn prevention steps, and understand their rights to health information and privacy — built as a Digital Public Good under an open-source licence.

Health topics

Plain-language guidance on the conditions affecting communities across Sierra Leone, drawn from public health guidance.

01 / TOPICS

Malaria

Spread by mosquito bites, common in the rainy season. Early signs include fever, chills, headache and body aches. Sleeping under a treated net and clearing standing water helps prevent it.

SDG 3

Cholera & water safety

Caused by unsafe drinking water and poor sanitation. Watery diarrhoea and vomiting are danger signs. Boil or chlorinate water, wash hands often, and seek care quickly to avoid dehydration.

SDG 6

Maternal & child health

Regular antenatal visits, skilled birth attendance, and full childhood vaccination protect mothers and babies. Visit your nearest Peripheral Health Unit (PHU) for free maternal and under-5 services.

SDG 3

Immunisation

Vaccines protect children against measles, polio, tetanus, and more. Keep your child's health card up to date and attend every scheduled visit — vaccination is free at government health facilities.

SDG 3

Hygiene & sanitation

Handwashing with soap at key moments — before eating, after the toilet, after handling waste — is one of the cheapest ways to stop the spread of diarrhoeal disease and respiratory infections.

SDG 6

Outbreak alerts

Stay informed about disease outbreaks reported by district health teams. If you notice many people in your area falling ill with similar symptoms, report it to your nearest health facility.

SDG 3

Your rights & this tool's licence

How this Digital Public Good handles your data, and the open terms it is shared under.

03 / LEGAL

No personal data collected

The symptom guide runs entirely in your browser. Nothing you select is sent to a server, stored, or shared with any third party.

Open-source licence (MIT)

This tool is released under the MIT License. Anyone may use, copy, modify, and share it — including health organisations, schools, and developers — provided the original copyright notice is kept.

Open data formats

Health topic content is structured for easy reuse in JSON, so other organisations can adapt or translate it for their own platforms.

Right to accurate information

You have the right to receive clear, factual health information. This tool avoids exaggeration and is reviewed against public health guidance.

Right to seek care without judgement

Information here is provided neutrally. Seeking medical help for any symptom, including reproductive and maternal health, is your right.

Accessibility

The tool is designed to be usable with a keyboard, screen reader, and on low-cost mobile devices, recognising unequal access to technology.
